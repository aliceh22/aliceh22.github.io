vocal fold paralysis

In 2017, I worked as a Research Assistant at the Bristol Robotics Laboratory investigating dynamic solutions to vocal fold paralysis. The study is focused on unilateral paresis in which one side of a person's vocal chords is unable to move causing potential difficulties with talking and breathing. Current solutions are static and have limited capabilities. Our group initiated ongoing investigations into alternative solutions using soft robotics.

2016

feel the music

feel the music

Music is a key component of my everyday life and something I treasure. For my Masters thesis I decided to explore how I could make music more accessible to those with hearing impairments, who cannot experience music acoustically. To do this I investigated the use of sensory substitution to engender the same affective response in a user via haptics as they would have when listening to the piece of music.

I designed, built and tested a wearable tactile interface in order to experiment with ideas. I generated mappings from audio tracks to vibration patterns, and assessed by how well they engendered the same affective response in the user as the music did. Two papers from my results are pending publication.

See the poster or the report to find out the results. Feel free to contact me for further information.

quadruped
motion

quadruped motion

As part of a scholarship awarded to me by Bristol University, I chose to design and create a robot horse that could perform the four distinct gaits; walk, trot, canter and gallop. Horses move between the gaits for maximum efficiency according to their speed. However, in quadruped robots, simpler gaits are used to suit the mechanics available. I wanted to experiment with mimicking natural locomotion.

Using frame-by-frame analysis of horse locomotion footage I gathered data for the phase difference between all four legs. This was used to control the motors in the robot. The greatest challenge was to create transition patterns between each gait.

The robot was built using Arduino Motor Shields and stepper motors to control the leg movement. The casing was made with laser-cut acrylic, 3D printed connections and metal gears turned on a lathe.

pre - 2014

human - robot interaction

human - robot interaction

Bristol, 2014. With the Departments of Psychology and Computer Science at Bristol University I completed a ten week research placement investigating the best method for locomoting robots to indicate their intended motion to the humans in their surroundings.

I worked with a partner to collect data for this project. We used 3D motion capture techonology to track 20 participants' movements as they negotiated a space with a robot, while the robot displayed different indications of its motion.

The paper published from this research: 'A Computational Model of Human-Robot Spatial Interactions Based on a Qualitative Trajectory Calculus'.

portfolio management

portfolio management

London, 2013. In the summer of my first year at University I worked with the Lloyds Portfolio Management team in their London HQ. Their role is to manage the product relationships between the bank and other financial insitutions, analysing large portfolios of products. A highly skilled team to be part of, I enjoyed learning about their analytical processes and how changes in the economy affect their work.

My main project was the development of a new proposal system for colleagues to use for assessing portfolios by their products and risk-factors. I also supported the team by updating other systems, taking minutes and organising the daily meetings as well as supporting my boss and gaining an understanding of his role as a manager of this highly-skilled and experienced team.

lloyds scholars program

lloyds scholars program

In 2012 I joined the Lloyds Scholars Program. The scheme, created by Lloyds Banking Group, is designed to support selected students from lower-income households through their undergraduate programs by providing them with mentorship, internships, skills-based events and encouraging them to volunteer in their local community.

The application process was rigorous but the scheme well worth it and I am very grateful for the experiences I had as a scholar. I lived and worked in two vibrant cities, gained a network of friends outside my University and was connected with opportunities I wouldn't have otherwise had. During the four years, I did over 400 hours of volunteering in and around Bristol which you can read about here. Find out about the internships in portfolio management and SME Scotland on this page.
