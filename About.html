alice haynes

Roboticist · Mathematician · Artist



Currently studying towards my PhD at the Bristol Robotics Lab, I work with supervisor Jonathan Rossiter in the Soft Robotics group, investigating novel approaches to environmental and agricultural robotics. My vision for robotics in these fields are organic, earthy, wholesome robots, that live and die in the environment, the purpose of their life being to benefit both humans and the planet.

I believe in research being open to the public and strive to find opportunities to share our work. This year we showcased our Soft Robotics research to the public at Research Without Borders and FUTURES @ We the Curious. I am also working part time at the Knowle West Media Centre looking at ways to engage with the local community via a series of workshops. This will include creating a piece of robotic art for their new makerspace, The Factory. Find out more about our collaboration here.

previous + education

In 2016, I graduated top of my year from my Masters in Engineering Mathematics with First Class honours. During my degree, I built a robotic horse, designed and fabricated a wearable tactile device and delved into some freaky chaotic nonlinear dynamics among other projects. I also worked in research, finance and volunteered with over eight charities I care about deeply in areas such as conservation, education and accessibility of the arts. After graduation I spent some time exploring, meeting people and learning how to build stuff, before returning to Bristol for my PhD.

One year into my research, I have helped build a robot to compete at the Robosoft Grand Challenge in Livorno, Italy, collaborated with Kyoto University in Japan on wearable haptic devices and attended the Cambridge Agri-Tech Conference on technology for agriculture. I am looking forward to what the next year will bring and getting hands-on with environmental robots.
