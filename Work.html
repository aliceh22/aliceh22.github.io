Lloyds Scholars Program
2012 - 2016

In 2012 I joined the Lloyds Scholars Program. The scheme, created by Lloyds Banking Group, is designed to support selected students from lower-income households through their undergraduate programs by providing them with mentorship, internships, skills-based events and encouraging them to volunteer in their local community.

The application process was rigorous but the scheme well worth it and I am very grateful for the experiences I had as a scholar. I lived and worked in two vibrant cities, gained a network of friends outside my University and was connected with opportunities I wouldn't have otherwise had. During the four years, I did over 400 hours of volunteering in and around Bristol which you can read about here. You can find out about the LLoyds Scholars Program on their website.

SME Scotland,
Edinburgh
2015

In Edinburgh I worked with the B.O.S. Scottish Small and Medium Enterprises team. They were a close-knit group that cared deeply about helping local businesses thrive and I appreciated their enthusiasm and guidance. I had the opportunity to work in each stage of the SME process; from meeting with clients (such as a hydroplant by Loch Lomond and a bakery near Fort William) to the analysis of their credit reports.

Human - Robot Interaction, Bristol
2014

With the Departments of Psychology and Computer Science at Bristol University I completed a ten week research placement investigating the best method for locomoting robots to indicate their intended motion to the humans in their surroundings.

I worked with a partner to collect data for this project. We used 3D motion capture techonology to track participants movements as they negotiated a space with a robot, while the robot displayed different indications of its motion.

The paper published from this research was: 'A Computational Model of Human-Robot Spatial Interactions Based on a Qualitative Trajectory Calculus'.
